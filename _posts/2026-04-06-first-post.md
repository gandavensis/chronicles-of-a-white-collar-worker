I am not sure how to start this, but I suppose I should simply begin by explaining what I intend to do with this project.

My goal is to report my perception of the technological and societal transformations that are currently taking place--that is, from the perspective of a reasonably well-educated, although rather mediocre, European STEM graduate born in 1999. Why? Well, first and foremost, because I like writing and I find it therapeutic. But on a second level, I confess that I also hope that my testimony can be useful for future historians. Not to study how the actual changes took place, but how they were perceived.

Now, I am fully aware that my perspective is simply “a perspective” and cannot possibly be equated with the general feeling--if there is such a thing as a “general feeling.” Nevertheless, I think that by explaining who I am, future historians can put my perspective into context. Hence the current name *diary of a white-collar worker*, although perhaps a more accurate title would be *diary of a young European aspiring to be a white-collar worker*.

I must clarify that I do not seek to be read by anyone, certainly not while I am alive. I am a rather shy person who does not like to be in the spotlight. More importantly, I am aware that I am not a special individual. I do not think that I am particularly gifted, and I am sure that my perspective is limited and biased. So why would I think that my perspective could be valuable for future historians? Well, it is precisely because I am no one special that I believe my testimony could be interesting to future historians.

It seems to me that throughout history, it has mainly been powerful and successful individuals who had the resources and the time to write down their perspectives. Moreover, human historians have a limited capacity to attend to all sources, and they probably tend to prioritize--understandably--the testimonies of relevant people. However, perhaps in the future, with the help of technology, historians may be able to develop a more nuanced and complete vision of how people felt while those transformations were taking place. Perhaps large models could be trained on millions of different perspectives from different individuals. My voice would simply be one more among millions.
